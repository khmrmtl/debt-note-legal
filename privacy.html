Debt Note

Privacy Policy

Effective date: 1 May 2026 · Last updated: 1 May 2026

The short version

  • Debt Note is offline-first. Your data never leaves your device.
  • There are no user accounts, no logins, and no servers operated by us.
  • We do not use analytics, advertising, tracking, or third-party SDKs that phone home.
  • We do not collect, see, or have access to your name, contacts, debts, photos, or any information you enter in the app.
  • Uninstalling the app deletes your data from your device.

1. Who we are

Debt Note ("the app", "we", "us", "our") is a personal-use mobile application developed and published by Khmer Motal, an individual developer based in Baguio City, Philippines. Debt Note is a personal record-keeping tool that helps users track informal debts owed to and by them, and generate receipt-style payment reminders to share through the user's own messaging apps.

This Privacy Policy explains how Debt Note handles your information. It applies to the Debt Note mobile app on iOS and Android.

2. Information we do not collect

Debt Note is designed around a strict principle: your data stays on your device. Specifically, we do not collect, transmit, store, sell, share, or have any access to:

  • Your name, email address, phone number, or any account identifier
  • Your phone contacts, even if you grant the app permission to read them
  • Names, amounts, notes, dates, or any details about debts or people you record in the app
  • Photos, screenshots, or attachments you add to debt entries
  • Receipt images you generate or share
  • Your device location or precise geolocation
  • Your device identifier (IDFA, AAID), advertising ID, or fingerprint
  • App usage analytics, session data, screen views, taps, or interaction events
  • Crash logs or diagnostic data sent to us
  • Your IP address (the app does not make network requests to any server we operate)

Debt Note has no backend server. We have no database in which your information could be stored, even if we wanted to.

3. Information stored locally on your device

The app stores the following information in a private local database on your device, accessible only to the app itself:

  • People you add to the app (name, optional photo or initials, optional contact reference)
  • Debt entries (amount, currency, date, optional note, optional photo attachments, interest settings)
  • Payment records logged against each debt
  • A reminder log (when you sent a reminder for a given debt)
  • Your preferences (display name, language, currency, theme, notification settings)

This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly export it (see Section 7) or share a generated receipt image (see Section 6). We have no access to it at any time.

5. No third-party services that collect your data

Debt Note does not integrate any third-party services that collect, process, or transmit personal data. In particular, the app does not use:

  • Analytics SDKs (e.g. Google Analytics, Firebase Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, PostHog)
  • Crash reporting that transmits data off-device (e.g. Crashlytics, Sentry, Bugsnag)
  • Advertising SDKs or ad networks
  • Attribution or marketing-tracking SDKs
  • Cloud sync, cloud backup, or remote database services
  • Social-login or single-sign-on providers
  • Payment-processing SDKs operated by us

6. Sharing receipts through your own messaging apps

The app's core feature lets you generate a receipt-style payment-reminder image entirely on your device, then share it through the native share sheet provided by your operating system. When you choose to share a receipt:

  • You select the destination app yourself (e.g. Messenger, SMS, Viber, Telegram, Instagram, email).
  • The receipt image is handed off to that app by your operating system. Debt Note does not intercept or transmit it.
  • Once a receipt leaves Debt Note, it becomes subject to the privacy policy of the app you shared it through, and to anyone you sent it to.

We have no visibility into which apps you share to or who receives your receipts.

7. App Store, Google Play, and purchase data

Debt Note is distributed through the Apple App Store and Google Play. When you purchase or install the app:

  • Apple or Google handles the payment and the download.
  • Apple and Google may collect certain information about your purchase and device, governed by their own privacy policies — Apple Privacy Policy and Google Privacy Policy.
  • We do not receive personally identifying information about you from these stores. We receive only aggregated, anonymized sales reports (e.g. "X units sold in country Y").

8. Backups, exports, and data you choose to share

You can manually export your data as CSV files for your own backup or record-keeping. These files are written only to a location you choose, on your device or storage you control (e.g. iCloud Drive, Google Drive, a USB transfer). We do not have access to them. If you upload a backup file to a cloud service, that service's privacy policy applies to the file there.

9. Children's privacy

Debt Note is not directed to children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction). The app is rated for general audiences but is intended for adults and older minors managing personal lending. Because the app does not collect any personal information from any user, we do not knowingly collect information from children. If you believe a child has somehow provided information through the app and you are concerned, please note that all data is stored locally on the child's device and can be removed by uninstalling the app or clearing app data.

10. Your rights

Because we do not collect or hold any of your personal data on our systems, most data-subject rights (access, deletion, correction, portability) are exercised directly on your device:

  • Access & portability: All your data is visible inside the app. Use the in-app CSV export to obtain a copy.
  • Correction: Edit any entry directly in the app.
  • Deletion: Delete entries individually in the app, or uninstall the app and clear its storage to remove everything.
  • Withdraw consent: Revoke any device permission (Contacts, Photos, Notifications) in your device settings at any time.

10.1 European Economic Area, United Kingdom, and Switzerland (GDPR / UK GDPR)

If you are located in the EEA, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and equivalent laws, including the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, port, and object to processing of your personal data, and the right to lodge a complaint with your local data-protection authority. Because Debt Note does not collect or process your personal data on any server, these rights are effectively exercised by you on your own device, as described above. We act as neither a controller nor a processor of any personal data of yours that you input into the app.

10.2 California (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act, as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act, gives you rights to know, delete, correct, and limit the use of your personal information, and to not be discriminated against for exercising these rights. We do not sell or share your personal information, and we do not collect it in the first place. We have not disclosed personal information for a business or commercial purpose in the preceding 12 months because we do not collect any.

10.3 Philippines (Data Privacy Act of 2012)

If you are a data subject under Republic Act No. 10173 (Data Privacy Act of 2012), you have rights to be informed, to object, to access, to rectification, to erasure or blocking, to damages, to data portability, and to file complaints with the National Privacy Commission. Because we do not collect or process your personal information on any system we operate, these rights are exercised on your device.

11. International users

Debt Note is offered globally. Because no personal data is transmitted to us or stored on any server we operate, no cross-border data transfer takes place between you and us. Data you store in the app remains in the country in which your device is located.

12. Security

Your data is protected by the security mechanisms of your device's operating system (iOS or Android), including app-sandbox isolation. We recommend you protect your device with a passcode, biometric lock, and up-to-date software. Because we do not transmit your data anywhere, the security of that data is in your hands.

The "Last updated" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision. Material changes will be communicated through an in-app notice or an updated app release.
